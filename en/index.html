What is ESN?

The Erasmus Student Network (ESN) is the biggest student association in Europe. It was born in 1989 with the goal to support and promote student mobility.

It is present in


More than 800 Higher Education Institutions


40 different countries

There are more than 15,000 active volunteers in ESN, offering their services to about 200,000 incoming students every year.

The mission of the network is to foster student mobility in Higher Education, and to provide opportunities for cultural understanding.


The Principle of ESN: "Students Helping Students"

ESN in Greece

Greece has been part of the Erasmus Student Network since 1991, when the first local Greek ESN section was founded.

Currently, there are 22 local ESN sections with more than 200 members, active in the biggest Universities of the country, in Athens and Thessaloniki, as well as in Patras, Ioannina, Crete, Komotini, Lamia, Larissa and the Aegean.

The Greek ESN sections receive in total more than

2,500

incoming international students every year.

Thanks to its multitude of activities for the orientation of incoming students and the preparation of outgoing students, ESN Greece is proud to have the full support of the State Scholarships Foundation (ΙΚΥ).

The Crete Trip

The Crete Trip is organized yearly by ESN Greece in Crete and it is the largest Erasmus event in Greece. It is one of the most popular activities in the Network on an international level, and it is addressed at incoming students all over Greece. It is a four-day trip packed with activities, which gives international students the chance to meet the culture of the island of Crete, and acts as the "peak-point" of their Erasmus.
